## 2. Era Map

### 2010-2012: Discovery, Ideology, Thin Markets

Signals:
- Bitcoin appears as a cypherpunk and monetary experiment.
- Early users mine, trade OTC, experiment with wallets, and learn by losing.
- Market infrastructure is fragile: early exchanges, PayPal/Liberty Reserve routes, thin liquidity, software bugs, primitive security.

Core lessons:
- Thin markets create huge upside and huge operational risk.
- Early edge comes from technical curiosity, custody discipline, and willingness to endure ambiguity.
- High-yield claims like BTCST can survive while inflow exists, then fail under redemption pressure.

Anchors:
- Early miner memoir, BTCST, GLBSE, Bitcoinica: 584-588.
- Bitcoin basics and early monetary framing: 52-59, 1051-1156.

### 2013-2014: First Bubble, ASIC Industrialization, MtGox

Signals:
- ASIC mining creates a real industrial cost base.
- Mining manufacturers and exchange venues become powerful intermediaries.
- MtGox exposes the gap between protocol robustness and custody fragility.

Core lessons:
- BTC can remain intact while centralized institutions fail.
- A price gap between venues can indicate withdrawal risk and insolvency risk.
- Industrial capex can support belief through sunk cost and forced holding.

Anchors:
- MtGox opacity and reserve doubt: 85-90.
- Early mining and ASIC transition: 584-588.
- Mining ecology and later industry narratives: 678-684, 890, 1202.

### 2015-2016: Survival, Infrastructure, Ideological Sorting

Signals:
- The market cools, builders continue, ideological tribes harden.
- Wallet security, full nodes, and protocol debates matter more than headline price.
- The industry learns that survival through winter creates reputational capital.

Core lessons:
- Bear markets reveal who builds, who stores keys, who keeps liquidity.
- Users who outsource custody and judgment carry hidden counterparty risk.
- Long-cycle assets gain strength from repeated survival.

Anchors:
- Full node and self-custody: 19-23, 170-211, 734, 905.
- Bitcoin investor psychology: 141-211, 236.

### 2017: ICO Mania and Fork War

Signals:
- ICO lowers public fundraising thresholds.
- Ethereum becomes the settlement asset for a fundraising boom.
- BCH/blocksize war tests hashpower, exchanges, naming, social consensus, and user legitimacy.

Core lessons:
- Tokenized fundraising can improve liquidity and transparency while expanding fraud surface.
- The real security of a chain includes users, nodes, developers, exchanges, symbols, and narrative legitimacy.
- "More features" can become a trap when the project captures all upside and leaves users weak incentives.

Anchors:
- ICO as stock-like future: 1396-1403.
- BTC vs BCH naming and social consensus: 50, 633, 889, 988-998, 1440-1444.
- POS/POW debates: 98-108, 1426.

### 2018-2019: Bear Market, Mode Coins, Stablecoin Experiments

Signals:
- ICO aftershocks expose weak teams and broken token economics.
- Mode coins compress speculation into "资金盘 + concept + market making".
- Algorithmic stablecoins reveal weak peg incentives and debt without a payer.

Core lessons:
- When every event is bullish, narrative has become distribution infrastructure.
- A stablecoin debt instrument needs a real payer, reserve, or enforceable claim.
- Projects that depend on user belief alone face a confidence cliff.

Anchors:
- Mode coins: 541-544.
- Algorithmic stablecoin bonds: 458-461.
- Bull-market all-news-is-bullish logic: 1185-1187.

### 2020: DeFi Summer and Smart Contract Finance

Signals:
- DeFi turns financial contracts into composable open systems.
- Liquidity mining uses emissions to bootstrap TVL.
- Contract audits prove partial safety; front-end, admin, oracle, bridge, human, and financial risks remain.

Core lessons:
- Yield quality depends on the payer: fees, emissions, leverage, or new users.
- Composability spreads both upside and contagion.
- Admin powers, front-end dependencies, and time locks deserve source-level verification.

Anchors:
- DeFi safety taxonomy: 67-78.
- Return-rate thinking: 509, 759, 1244.

### 2021: Institutional Peak, NFT, Mining Shutdown, Nation-State Bitcoin

Signals:
- BTC reaches institutional attention.
- NFT converts digital status and illiquid culture into tradable assets.
- China mining shutdown tests hashpower mobility.
- El Salvador tests sovereign Bitcoin adoption.

Core lessons:
- Culture can financialize when ownership, status, and liquidity meet.
- Mining is mobile in the long run, policy-sensitive in the short run.
- Nation-state adoption adds legitimacy and political risk together.

Anchors:
- NFT and tulip framing: 91-98, 1182-1185, 1230-1239.
- Sichuan/mining policy: 681-684.
- El Salvador: 474, 1366.

### 2022: Collapse of Leverage, Custody, and Reflexive Balance Sheets

Signals:
- Stablecoin, lending, fund, and exchange collapses expose circular collateral and hidden leverage.
- Counterparty risk becomes the central risk.
- "Proof of reserves" enters mainstream exchange due diligence.

Core lessons:
- Exchange risk repeats MtGox with bigger balance sheets.
- Yield and leverage chains break at the weakest liquidity gate.
- Self-custody and transparent reserves become survival requirements.

PDF support:
- The anthology has pre-2022 frameworks that explain this pattern: MtGox (85-90), DeFi safety (67-78), algorithmic stablecoin debt (458-461), custody caution (864).

### 2023-2024: L2, Restaking, Points, Solana/Meme Revival

Signals:
- Attention rotates from DeFi yield to points, restaking, L2s, modular infra, and meme factories.
- Meme coins act as rapid-liquidity cultural options.
- L1 ecosystems use sub-assets to refresh ROI and keep liquidity internal.

Core lessons:
- Points are delayed token promises; score them as debt with uncertain conversion.
- Meme factories are splitting plates: the base asset can benefit while many sub-assets die.
- The right question is whether the ecosystem creates enough ROI above market beta to keep participants engaged.

PDF support:
- Three-plate theory: 347-359.
- NFT fractionalization and altcoin-like liquidity: 1182-1185.
- Mode coin mechanics: 541-544.
